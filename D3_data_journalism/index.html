Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income


Poverty & Behavioral Risk Factors

The above chart illustrates the positive correlation between poverty and risky behavior. Residents of states with a higher percentage of the population below the poverty line are also more likely to be obese, smoke or lack healthcare.

Median Household Income & Behavioral Risk Factors

Given the above, there is naturally a negative correlation between median household income and risky behavior. As a state's median household income increases, the percentage of residents who are obese, smoke, or without healthcare decrease.

Median Age & Behavioral Risk Factors

The only clear correlation between median age and behavioral risk factors is between age and healthcare. The older the median age of a state's population is, the more likely they are to have healthcare. There does not appear to be a significant correlation between median age and obesity or smoking.
